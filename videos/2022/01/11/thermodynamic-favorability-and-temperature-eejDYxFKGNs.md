## Description

The temperature conditions under which a process is thermodynamically favored (ΔG° is negative) can be predicted from the signs of ΔH° and ΔS°. When ΔH° is negative and ΔS° is positive, the process is favored at all temperatures. When ΔH° is positive and ΔS° is negative, the process is favored at no temperature. When ΔH° and ΔS° are both negative, the process is favored only at low temperatures. When ΔH° and ΔS° are both positive, the process is favored only at high temperatures.
