## Description

According to the Boltzmann equation, entropy is a measure of the number of microstates available to a system. The number of available microstates increases when matter becomes more dispersed, such as when a liquid changes into a gas or when a gas is expanded at constant temperature. It also increases when energy becomes more dispersed, which occurs with an increase in temperature.
