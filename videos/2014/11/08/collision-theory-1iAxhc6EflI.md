## Description

Collision theory states that molecules must collide to react. For most reactions, however, only a small fraction of collisions produce a reaction. In order for a collision to be successful, the reactant molecules must collide both with sufficient energy to overcome the activation energy barrier and in the proper orientation to form any new bonds in the products.
