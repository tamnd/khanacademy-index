The Beer–Lambert law relates the absorption of light by a solution to the properties of the solution according to the following equation: A = εbc, where ε is the molar absorptivity of the absorbing species, b is the path length, and c is the concentration of the absorbing species. In this video, we'll use the Beer–Lambert law to calculate the concentration of KMnO₄ in an unknown solution.
