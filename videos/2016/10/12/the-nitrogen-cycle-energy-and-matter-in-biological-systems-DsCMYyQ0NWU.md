The nitrogen cycle is a crucial process that converts atmospheric nitrogen into a form that plants and other organisms can use. This process, known as nitrogen fixation, is primarily carried out by bacteria. Nitrogen is essential for life, forming a key component in amino acids, ATP, and DNA. The cycle also includes the return of nitrogen to the atmosphere through decomposition.
