## Description

The equilibrium constant, K, describes the relative amounts of reaction species at equilibrium. The expression for K is equal to the concentrations (or partial pressures) of the products raised to their stoichiometric coefficients divided by the concentrations (or partial pressures) of the reactants raised to their stoichiometric coefficients. The reaction quotient, Q, has the same form as K but describes the relative amounts of reaction species at any point in time.
