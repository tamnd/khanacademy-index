Strong acids (such as HCl, HBr, HI, HNO₃, HClO₄, and H₂SO₄) ionize completely in water to produce hydronium ions. The concentration of H₃O⁺ in a strong acid solution is therefore equal to the initial concentration of the acid. For example, a solution of 0.1 M HNO₃ contains 0.1 M H₃O⁺ and has a pH of 1.0.
