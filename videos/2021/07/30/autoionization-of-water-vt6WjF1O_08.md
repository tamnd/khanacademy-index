In the autoionization of water, a proton is transferred from one water molecule to another to produce a hydronium ion (H₃O⁺) and a hydroxide ion (OH⁻). The equilibrium expression for this reaction is Kw = [H₃O⁺][OH⁻], where Kw is the autoionization constant for water. At 25°C, the value of Kw is 1.0 x 10⁻¹⁴. In pure water, the concentrations of H₃O⁺ and OH⁻ are equal, and the water is considered to be neutral.
