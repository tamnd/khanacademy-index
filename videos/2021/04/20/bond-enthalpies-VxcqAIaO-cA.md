The enthalpy of a bond is the enthalpy change that occurs when 1 mole of a particular bond is broken in the gas phase. Since energy is required to break a chemical bond, bond enthalpies are always reported as positive values. For any chemical reaction, the estimated change in enthalpy is the sum of the bond enthalpies of the bonds broken minus the sum of the bond enthalpies of the bonds formed.
