Ecological interactions explain how organisms share limited resources in ecosystems. Competition, predation, herbivory, and symbioses (such as mutualism, commensalism, and parasitism) all affect survival and population size. Different strategies (such as using resources in different ways) allow many species to live together in various ecosystems.
