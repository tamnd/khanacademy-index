Water’s unique properties make it a powerful solvent, allowing it to dissolve many substances and form solutions. These solutions can conduct electricity if they contain electrolytes—solutes that break into charged particles in water. Using a conductivity tester, we can see that soluble substances like salt allow electricity to flow, while insoluble ones do not.
