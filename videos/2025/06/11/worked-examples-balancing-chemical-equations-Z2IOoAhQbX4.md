Balancing chemical equations ensures they follow the law of conservation of matter—atoms are neither created nor destroyed, only rearranged. We adjust coefficients, not subscripts, to equalize atom counts on both sides of the equation. Coefficients must be in their simplest whole-number ratio to accurately represent the reaction.
