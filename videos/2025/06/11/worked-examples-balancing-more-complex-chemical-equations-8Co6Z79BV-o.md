Balancing chemical equations ensures the same number of each atom appears on both sides, following the law of conservation of matter. Adjust only coefficients—not subscripts—to achieve balance. When a formula has parentheses with a subscript, the subscript applies to every element contained in the parenthesis. Simplify coefficients to the lowest whole-number ratio, and group complex particles like sulfate (SO₄) to make counting easier!
