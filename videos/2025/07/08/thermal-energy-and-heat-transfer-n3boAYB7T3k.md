When a warmer object meets a cooler one, energy flows until they reach the same temperature, also known as thermal equilibrium. This transfer of energy between systems is what we call heat. Temperature measures the average kinetic energy or motion of these particles. All matter contains moving particles with kinetic energy, which contributes to its thermal energy.
