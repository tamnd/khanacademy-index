Organisms need specific abiotic factors for development, survival, and reproduction, and have a tolerance range for each. The fundamental niche of an organism represents the environmental conditions in which it could exist, but its realized niche is often restricted by species interactions and resource availability. Species vary from specialists, with narrow niches, to generalists, with broad niches.
