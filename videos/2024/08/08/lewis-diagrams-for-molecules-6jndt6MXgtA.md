Lewis diagrams (or Lewis structures) are models that show how atoms are bonded together in a molecule and the arrangement of valence electrons around the atoms in that molecule. They provide a visual representation that helps us understand molecular bonding, visualize molecular shapes, and predict chemical reactivity.
