A Lewis diagram shows how the valence electrons are distributed around the atoms in a molecule. Shared pairs of electrons are drawn as lines between atoms, while lone pairs of electrons are drawn as dots next to atoms. When constructing a Lewis diagram, keep in mind the octet rule, which refers to the tendency of atoms to gain, lose, or share electrons until they are surrounded by eight valence electrons (an "octet").
