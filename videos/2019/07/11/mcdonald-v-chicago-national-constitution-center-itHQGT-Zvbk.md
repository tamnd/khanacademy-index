A deep dive into McDonald v. Chicago, a 2010 Supreme Court case that ruled that the Second Amendment's right to keep and bear arms for self-defense in one's home is applicable to the states through the Fourteenth Amendment. In this video, Kim Kutz Elliott discusses the case with litigator Alan Gura and Elizabeth Wydra, President of the Constitutional Accountability Center.
