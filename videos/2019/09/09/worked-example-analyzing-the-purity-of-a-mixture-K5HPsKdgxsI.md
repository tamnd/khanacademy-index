Elemental analysis can be used to analyze the purity of a sample. For example, a pure sample of NaCl should contain 61% chlorine by mass. If elemental analysis tells us that the sample actually contains 73% chlorine by mass, this suggests that our sample has been contaminated by a compound containing a higher mass percent of chlorine.
