All living organisms share a common ancestor, evidenced by cellular similarities like DNA, RNA, and glycolysis. Evolutionary trees depict these relationships, with branches representing different species. Eukaryotes, a distinct group, possess membrane-bound organelles, linear chromosomes, and introns. DNA and RNA sequencing refines our understanding of these connections, enabling more precise evolutionary tree construction.
